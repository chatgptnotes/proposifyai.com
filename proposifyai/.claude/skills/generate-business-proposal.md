# Generate Business Proposal (Master Skill)

Generate professional business proposals for any type of product, service, or solution. This is the master skill that covers general proposal structure and can be customized for any industry or offering.

## Context & Purpose

**When to use:** Create comprehensive business proposals for any industry, service, or product when specialized skills don't apply.

- Service agreements
- Partnership proposals
- Grant applications
- RFP responses

## Universal Proposal Structure

### 1. Cover Page
**Essential Elements:**
- Company logo and branding
- Proposal title
- Client name
- Prepared by (your company)
- Date
- Reference number
- Total investment (prominently displayed)
- Tagline or project summary

**Design Options:**
- **Formal:** Clean header with logos and info
- **Creative:** Full-page branded design with graphics
- **Minimalist:** Simple text-based cover

### 2. Executive Summary (1 page max)
**Purpose:** Give decision-makers the key information

**Include:**
- Problem statement (what challenge does client face?)
- Proposed solution (how will you solve it?)
- Key benefits (what value do you deliver?)
- Investment required
- Timeline/duration
- Why choose us (unique value proposition)

**Template:**
```
[CLIENT NAME] faces [PROBLEM/CHALLENGE]. Our proposed [SOLUTION] will
deliver [KEY BENEFIT 1], [KEY BENEFIT 2], and [KEY BENEFIT 3]. With an
investment of [AMOUNT] over [TIMEFRAME], we will [SPECIFIC OUTCOME].

Our team brings [UNIQUE QUALIFICATION] and has successfully [PROOF POINT].
```

### 3. About Your Company
**Purpose:** Build credibility and trust

**Include:**
- Company overview (1-2 paragraphs)
- Years in business
- Core expertise
- Team size and structure
- Notable clients or projects
- Certifications/awards
- Company values/mission

**Template:**
```
[COMPANY NAME] is a [LOCATION]-based [INDUSTRY] company specializing in
[CORE OFFERINGS]. Since [YEAR], we've helped [NUMBER] clients achieve
[OUTCOME] through [APPROACH].

Our team of [NUMBER] professionals includes [KEY ROLES] with expertise in
[SKILLS]. We're proud to serve clients including [NOTABLE CLIENTS] and
have been recognized for [ACHIEVEMENTS].
```

### 4. Understanding Your Needs
**Purpose:** Show you understand the client's situation

**Include:**
- Current challenges documented
- Pain points identified
- Client goals and objectives
- Success criteria
- Constraints (budget, timeline, resources)

**This section shows:**
- You listened to the client
- You understand their context
- Your solution is tailored, not generic

### 5. Proposed Solution
**Purpose:** Explain exactly what you'll deliver

**Structure:**
- Solution overview
- Key components/modules
- Features and functionality
- Technology/methodology
- Deliverables list
- Timeline/phases

**Be specific:**
- ❌ "We'll build a mobile app"
- ✅ "Native iOS and Android apps with [features], deployed to App Store and Play Store"

### 6. Scope of Work
**Purpose:** Define what's included and what's not

**Include:**
**In Scope:**
- Detailed task breakdown
- Deliverables per phase
- Meetings/communication
- Testing and QA
- Training
- Documentation

**Out of Scope:**
- Items explicitly excluded
- Future phase items
- Optional add-ons
- Client responsibilities

**Change Management:**
- How scope changes are handled
- Change request process
- Impact on timeline/budget

### 7. Methodology/Approach
**Purpose:** Explain how you'll execute

**Examples:**
- **Software Development:** Agile/Scrum, sprints, user stories
- **Consulting:** Discovery, analysis, recommendations, implementation
- **Design:** Research, concept, design, iterate, finalize
- **Marketing:** Strategy, creation, execution, measurement

**Include:**
- Phases/stages
- Activities in each phase
- Client involvement points
- Review/approval gates
- Quality assurance process

### 8. Timeline & Milestones
**Purpose:** Set expectations for delivery

**Format Options:**

**Gantt Chart Style:**
```
Phase 1: Discovery & Planning (Weeks 1-2)
├─ Kickoff meeting
├─ Requirements gathering
└─ Project plan approval

Phase 2: Design & Development (Weeks 3-8)
├─ Design approval (Week 4)
├─ Development checkpoint (Week 6)
└─ QA testing (Week 8)

Phase 3: Launch (Weeks 9-10)
├─ User acceptance testing
├─ Deployment
└─ Training & handover
```

**Table Format:**
| Milestone | Description | Deadline | Deliverable |
|-----------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Kickoff | Project initiation | Week 1 | Kickoff doc |
| Design | UI/UX completed | Week 4 | Design files |
| Beta | Testable version | Week 8 | Beta access |
| Launch | Production release | Week 10 | Live system |

### 9. Investment & Pricing
**Purpose:** Clearly communicate costs

**Pricing Models:**

**Fixed Price:**
```
Total Project Cost: $XX,XXX

Breakdown:
- Discovery & Planning: $X,XXX
- Design: $X,XXX
- Development: $XX,XXX
- Testing: $X,XXX
- Deployment: $X,XXX
```

**Time & Materials:**
```
Estimated Budget: $XX,XXX - $XX,XXX

Rates:
- Senior Developer: $XXX/hour
- Designer: $XXX/hour
- Project Manager: $XXX/hour

Estimated Hours: XXX - XXX hours
```

**Monthly Retainer:**
```
Monthly Retainer: $X,XXX/month

Includes:
- XX hours of service per month
- Dedicated team access
- Priority support
- Monthly reporting
```

**Subscription/SaaS:**
```
Per User/Month: $XX
Per Location/Month: $XXX
Enterprise: Custom pricing

Annual commitment: 15% discount
```

### 10. Payment Terms
**Purpose:** Define payment schedule

**Common Structures:**

**Milestone-based:**
```
- 30% upon contract signing
- 40% upon Phase 1 completion
- 30% upon final delivery
```

**Monthly:**
```
- Equal monthly payments over project duration
- Invoice sent on 1st of month
- Payment due within 15 days
```

**Split Payment:**
```
- 50% upfront
- 50% upon completion
```

**Include:**
- Payment method (bank transfer, credit card, etc.)
- Late payment terms
- Refund policy
- Currency

### 11. Team & Expertise
**Purpose:** Introduce who will do the work

**Include:**
- Team structure
- Key personnel and roles
- Relevant experience
- Certifications/qualifications
- Availability/dedication

**Format:**
```
Project Team:
- [NAME], Project Manager (XX years experience)
  • Led XX similar projects
  • [Relevant certification]

- [NAME], Lead Developer (XX years experience)
  • Expert in [TECHNOLOGY]
  • Previously worked on [RELEVANT PROJECT]

- [NAME], Designer (XX years experience)
  • Award-winning [ACHIEVEMENT]
  • [Portfolio link]
```

### 12. Case Studies/Portfolio
**Purpose:** Prove you can deliver

**Include 2-3 relevant examples:**
```
Client: [NAME/INDUSTRY]
Challenge: [PROBLEM]
Solution: [WHAT WE DID]
Results: [QUANTIFIED OUTCOMES]
- [METRIC]: +XX%
- [METRIC]: -XX%
- [METRIC]: $XX,XXX saved
```

### 13. Terms & Conditions
**Purpose:** Legal protection and clarity

**Common Terms:**
- Contract duration
- Intellectual property rights
- Confidentiality
- Warranties and guarantees
- Limitation of liability
- Termination clause
- Dispute resolution
- Governing law

### 14. Next Steps & Acceptance
**Purpose:** Make it easy to say yes

**Include:**
- Clear call to action
- How to accept (signature, PO, email)
- Contact information
- Questions/clarifications process
- Proposal validity period

**Acceptance Template:**
```
To proceed with this proposal:

1. Sign and date the acceptance section below
2. Return signed copy via email to [EMAIL]
3. Initial payment of [AMOUNT] to be made within [X] days
4. Project kickoff scheduled within [X] days of payment

Questions? Contact [NAME] at [EMAIL] or [PHONE]

This proposal is valid until [DATE].
```

### 15. Appendices (Optional)
- Detailed technical specifications
- Additional case studies
- Team CVs
- Certifications
- Legal documents
- Third-party reports

## Design Principles

### Visual Hierarchy
1. **Headers:** Largest, bold, colored
2. **Subheaders:** Medium, bold
3. **Body:** Readable size (12-14px)
4. **Captions:** Smaller (10-11px)

### Color Usage
- **Primary:** Company brand color
- **Secondary:** Complementary color
- **Neutral:** Grays for backgrounds and text
- **Accent:** Highlight important info

### White Space
- Don't overcrowd pages
- Margins: 15-20mm
- Padding around sections
- Line spacing: 1.6-1.8

### Typography
- **Headers:** Sans-serif, bold
- **Body:** Readable font (Arial, Helvetica, Segoe UI)
- **Limit:** Max 2-3 font families

### Images & Graphics
- High quality (300 DPI for print)
- Relevant to content
- Proper attribution
- Icons for visual interest
- Charts for data visualization

## File Formats

### HTML (Recommended)
- Easy to share and view
- Consistent rendering
- Can be converted to PDF
- Interactive elements possible

### PDF
- Professional standard
- Can't be easily edited
- Print-ready
- Digital signatures supported

### Word/Google Docs
- Easy for client to comment
- Can be edited
- Version control challenges

## File Naming Convention
Format: `[ClientName]_[ProjectName]_Proposal_[Date].html`
Example: `Acme_Corp_Website_Redesign_Proposal_2025-12-26.html`

## Proposal Writing Tips

### Do:
- Use client's language and terminology
- Quantify benefits whenever possible
- Include social proof (testimonials, logos)
- Address objections proactively
- Make it scannable (bullets, subheads, charts)
- Proofread thoroughly
- Include clear next steps

### Don't:
- Use jargon without explanation
- Make promises you can't keep
- Include irrelevant information
- Copy/paste without customization
- Forget to update dates/names
- Overwhelm with too much detail
- Skip the executive summary
