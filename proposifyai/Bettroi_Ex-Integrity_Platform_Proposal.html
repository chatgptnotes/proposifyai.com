1. Executive Summary

Development of Bettroi Ex-Integrity (EX-INT), a comprehensive enterprise-grade software platform for digitizing end-to-end explosion-protected asset integrity management across hazardous areas in oil and gas, petrochemicals, chemical processing, FPSO, terminals, pipelines, and power generation facilities.

The solution addresses critical industry needs for compliance with IEC 60079 series standards (60079-14, 60079-17, 60079-19) and regulatory requirements (IECEx, ATEX 2014/34/EU), providing full lifecycle management of Ex-rated equipment from installation through inspection, maintenance, and audit. The platform integrates risk-based inspection (RBI) methodologies adapted from API 580/581 with ISO 14224 reliability taxonomy to optimize inspection strategies while maintaining safety-critical operations in Zones 0/1/2 and 20/21/22 classified areas.

Key capabilities include multi-tenant asset hierarchy management with Ex marking parser and certificate validation, configurable inspection checklists mapped to IEC clauses, offline-first Android mobile application for ATEX/IECEx certified devices with advanced evidence capture (photo/video/audio with chain-of-custody hashing), intelligent RBI engine with dynamic risk scoring and interval optimization, bi-directional CMMS integration (SAP PM, IBM Maximo, Infor EAM), comprehensive reporting and analytics with compliance tracking, and immutable audit trails meeting ISO 27001 and IEC 62443-4-1 security standards.

Business Value Proposition

  • 30% reduction in planned inspection hours through risk-based scheduling within 6 months
  • 98% evidence compliance for severity A/B findings with automated chain of custody
  • 95% offline sync success enabling continuous field operations in remote hazardous areas
  • Real-time regulatory compliance with automated certificate expiry tracking and SLA management
  • Zero critical security issues with enterprise-grade encryption, tamper resistance, and audit controls

The platform will be delivered in two phases: MVP (90 days) covering core multi-tenant infrastructure, asset register, offline mobile inspection capture, basic RBI, and SAP PM integration; followed by Phase 2 (90-120 days) adding advanced RBI with machine learning, route optimization, mobile annotation, webhooks, multilingual support, and external auditor portal.

This investment positions Bettroi as a leading provider of specialized Ex-asset integrity solutions for high-hazard industries, establishing a scalable SaaS platform capable of managing 1M+ assets across 10,000+ concurrent mobile sessions with 99.9% availability and sub-300ms API response times.

2. Scope of Work

In-scope

  • Multi-tenant Platform Core: Schema-per-tenant PostgreSQL architecture with tenant-aware middleware, per-tenant encryption keys (AWS KMS), tenant lifecycle management (suspend/export/purge), SAML 2.0/OIDC SSO with MFA (TOTP + device attestation), and granular RBAC with row-level security
  • Asset Management System: Hierarchical structure (Site→Area→Location→Zone→Equipment) with Ex marking string parser, IECEx/ATEX certificate validator, gas/dust group classification, T-class and IP rating tracking, parent/child assemblies, calibration scheduling, and comprehensive document vault with versioning
  • Inspection Management: Configurable checklists mapped to IEC 60079-17 clauses, support for Initial/Detailed/Close/Periodic/Visual/Sample inspections, defect taxonomy with A/B/C severity codes, multi-modal evidence capture (photo/video/audio/sketch/barcode/RFID), digital signatures with approval workflows, and bulk work-pack generation with route optimization
  • Risk-Based Inspection Engine: Configurable PoF/CoF weightings, 5×5 risk matrix with dynamic scoring, Bayesian updates from defect trends, automated interval recommendations with min/max guardrails, sampling percentage calculator, and dynamic backlog re-ranking by risk and access windows
  • Android Mobile Application (Offline-First): Native Kotlin app for ATEX/IECEx certified devices, encrypted SQLite with append-only event log, fast tag lookup via QR/Code-128/DataMatrix/RFID, camera capture with on-device annotation and redaction, voice-to-text notes, geofence and BLE beacon support, zone safety overlays, and conflict-resilient sync with vector clocks
  • CMMS/EAM Integration: Bi-directional adapters for SAP PM (OData), IBM Maximo (REST), and Infor EAM, automated work order creation from NCRs, attachment and labor actuals sync, webhook event subscriptions, retry policies, and rate limiting
  • Reporting & Analytics: Compliance dashboards (completion rates, overdue, defect density by zone), RBI analytics (risk distribution, interval shifts, cost vs. risk curves), productivity metrics (hours/asset, findings/shift, rework rate), automated certificate generation with QR codes and evidence index, scheduled exports (PDF/XLSX/CSV/JSON), and email delivery
  • Evidence & Document Vault: S3-compatible object storage with content hashing and chain of custody, certificate of conformity (CoC/ExTR) management, GA drawings and IOM storage with versioning, role-based retrieval, watermarking options, and 15-minute RPO backup strategy
  • Alerts & Workflow Automation: SLA timers for NCRs by severity, threshold alerts for overdue inspections and certificate expiry, multi-channel notifications (email, SMS via Twilio/MSG91, Teams/Slack integration), escalation ladders, and workflow state machines
  • Security & Compliance: TLS 1.2+ transport encryption, AES-256 at-rest encryption, OWASP MASVS mobile security, IEC 62443-4-1 SDLC practices, device jailbreak/root detection, AV scanning for uploads, EXIF scrubbing, immutable audit logs with before/after snapshots, and ISO 27001 controls
  • Deployment & DevOps: Kubernetes on AWS with auto-scaling, blue/green deployment strategy, CI/CD pipeline with SAST/DAST and SBOM, Terraform IaC, CloudFront CDN, WAF, OpenTelemetry observability, Prometheus/Grafana monitoring, ELK logging, and Sentry error tracking
  • Quality Assurance: ≥80% unit test coverage, API contract testing, Appium mobile UI automation, 4-week field UAT at pilot site with 2000+ assets, RBI backtesting against historical defects, performance testing for 10k concurrent sync bursts, and pen-testing before GA release

4. Technology Stack & Architecture

Frontend

  • Web Application: React 18 with Next.js 14 (App Router), TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, shadcn/ui components, React Query for state management, Recharts for data visualization
  • Progressive Web App: Service worker for offline asset registry lookup, IndexedDB for client-side caching

Mobile

  • Android: Kotlin native with Jetpack Compose, Room database (encrypted SQLite), WorkManager for background sync, CameraX for image capture, Ktor for HTTP client, Moshi for JSON parsing
  • Security: Android Keystore for encryption keys, SafetyNet Attestation API for device integrity, ProGuard obfuscation

Backend

  • Framework: Modular monolith architecture using Spring Boot 3 (Java 21) or .NET 8 (C#)
  • Services: Auth (OAuth2/SAML), Tenancy Manager, Asset Registry, Inspection Engine, RBI Calculator, Evidence Handler, Report Generator, CMMS Adapters, Notification Hub, Search Indexer, Sync Orchestrator
  • API: RESTful with OpenAPI 3.0 specification, GraphQL endpoint (optional) for complex queries, WebSocket for real-time updates

Data Layer

  • Primary Database: PostgreSQL 15+ with schema-per-tenant pattern, PostGIS for spatial data, pg_cron for scheduled jobs
  • Object Storage: AWS S3 or MinIO for media files, versioning enabled, lifecycle policies for archival
  • Cache: Redis 7+ for session management, rate limiting, and frequently accessed metadata
  • Search: OpenSearch (Elasticsearch fork) for full-text search on assets, findings, and documents
  • Time-Series (Optional): TimescaleDB extension for IoT sensor data if future OPC UA/MQTT integration enabled

Infrastructure

  • Cloud Provider: AWS (primary), multi-AZ deployment in selected region
  • Container Orchestration: Amazon EKS (Kubernetes 1.28+) with auto-scaling node groups
  • Load Balancing: Application Load Balancer (ALB) with TLS termination, NGINX ingress controller for path-based routing
  • CDN: CloudFront for static assets, edge caching for API responses in select endpoints
  • Security: AWS WAF with OWASP Core Rule Set, AWS Shield for DDoS protection, AWS Secrets Manager for credentials, KMS for encryption keys

DevOps & Observability

  • CI/CD: GitHub Actions or GitLab CI with automated testing, SAST (SonarQube), DAST (OWASP ZAP), SBOM generation (Syft), container scanning (Trivy)
  • Infrastructure as Code: Terraform for AWS resources, Helm charts for Kubernetes deployments, version-controlled and peer-reviewed
  • Monitoring: Prometheus for metrics collection, Grafana for visualization, alerting via PagerDuty/Opsgenie
  • Logging: ELK stack (Elasticsearch, Logstash, Kibana) or AWS CloudWatch Logs with structured JSON logging
  • Tracing: OpenTelemetry with Jaeger backend for distributed request tracing
  • Error Tracking: Sentry for backend and mobile error aggregation with release tracking

Integration Protocols

  • CMMS/EAM: OData v4 (SAP PM), REST APIs (Maximo, Infor EAM), webhook callbacks
  • Identity Providers: SAML 2.0, OIDC (OpenID Connect), LDAP/Active Directory
  • Messaging: SMTP (SendGrid/AWS SES), SMS (Twilio/MSG91), Microsoft Teams/Slack webhooks, optional WhatsApp Business API
  • Document Systems: Microsoft Graph API for SharePoint/OneDrive, AWS S3 API for object storage

Architecture Patterns

  • Multi-Tenancy: Schema-per-tenant for data isolation, tenant-aware middleware, per-tenant encryption keys
  • Offline Sync: Event sourcing with append-only log, vector clocks for conflict detection, CRDT (Conflict-free Replicated Data Types) for media sets, resumable uploads with chunking
  • Microservices (Modular Monolith): Domain-driven design with bounded contexts, clean architecture layers, eventual consistency via outbox pattern where applicable
  • Security: Zero-trust network model, principle of least privilege, defense in depth, immutable audit logs

10. Data Privacy & Security

Security Controls

  • Encryption: TLS 1.2+ for data in transit; AES-256 for data at rest in database, object storage, and mobile local storage
  • Key Management: AWS KMS with per-tenant encryption keys; automatic rotation every 90 days; hardware-backed keys on mobile devices (Android Keystore)
  • Authentication: SAML 2.0/OIDC SSO with MFA (TOTP + device attestation); password policy enforcing 12+ characters with complexity; account lockout after 5 failed attempts
  • Authorization: Granular RBAC with verb-level permissions (create, read, update, delete, approve); row-level security enforcing tenant ID isolation in database queries
  • Mobile Security: OWASP MASVS Level 2 compliance; SafetyNet Attestation for device integrity; jailbreak/root detection with blocking; encrypted SQLite with hardware-backed keys; certificate pinning for API calls
  • Application Security: SAST (SonarQube) and DAST (OWASP ZAP) in CI/CD pipeline; dependency vulnerability scanning (Trivy, Dependabot); SBOM generation and audit
  • Network Security: AWS WAF with OWASP Core Rule Set; DDoS protection via AWS Shield; VPC with private subnets for database and backend services; security groups with least-privilege rules
  • Audit Logging: Immutable logs with timestamp, user ID, tenant ID, action, before/after state; 7-year retention in tamper-evident storage (S3 Object Lock)
  • Media Hygiene: Antivirus scanning on upload (ClamAV or AWS GuardDuty Malware Protection); content-type validation; EXIF metadata scrubbing policy; content hashing (SHA-256) for chain of custody

Data Privacy

  • Data Minimization: No PII collected except business contact data (inspector name, email, phone) required for audit trail and notifications
  • Tenant Isolation: Schema-per-tenant architecture with row-level security; tenant data cannot be accessed across boundaries even via SQL injection
  • Right to Erasure: Tenant purge API with complete data deletion including backups within 30 days of request; audit log entry preserved for compliance
  • Cross-Border Data: Data residency configurable per tenant; AWS region selection during tenant provisioning; no automatic cross-region replication
  • Subprocessors: Documented list of third-party services (AWS, Twilio, SendGrid) with data processing agreements in place

Compliance Standards

  • ISO 27001: Security controls aligned with Annex A requirements
  • IEC 62443-4-1: Secure development lifecycle practices including threat modeling, secure coding guidelines, and security testing
  • OWASP MASVS: Mobile Application Security Verification Standard Level 2 for Android app
  • SOC 2 Type II (Future): Infrastructure and processes designed to support SOC 2 audit post-launch

12. Change Control

Change Request Process

  1. Identification: Client or Bettroi team member identifies need for scope change (new feature, modified requirement, technical constraint, regulatory update)
  2. Impact Analysis: Bettroi product manager and tech lead assess impact on:
    • Timeline: Delay to milestones, additional sprints required
    • Cost: Incremental effort in person-days, infrastructure changes
    • Risk: Technical feasibility, dependencies on other features
    • Quality: Test coverage, regression risk, performance impact
  3. Proposal: Bettroi drafts Change Request document with description, justification, impact summary, revised timeline, incremental cost estimate
  4. Review: Joint Change Control Board (CCB) meeting within 5 business days of CR submission; attendees include client product owner, Bettroi project manager, tech lead
  5. Decision: CCB approves, rejects, or defers CR; approved CRs trigger contract amendment for material changes (>$10K or >2 weeks impact)
  6. Execution: Approved CRs added to sprint backlog with priority; Bettroi updates project plan, budget tracking, and risk register

Change Categories

  • Clarifications: Minor requirement clarifications or bug fixes with no material impact on timeline/cost → Fast-tracked approval via email
  • Standard Changes: Small enhancements within existing epics (e.g., additional checklist field, new report column) → CCB review, may be absorbed or quoted as minor add-on
  • Major Changes: New features, significant rework, or architectural changes → Formal CR with contract amendment and revised SOW

Change Budget & Timeline
